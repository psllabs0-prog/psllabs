The supplement industry profits from complexity. Twelve-ingredient formulas. Rotating "stacks." Monthly breakthroughs. The research literature points in a different direction: a small number of well-characterized compounds, taken consistently, at disclosed doses.

## Start with the base

Foundation is designed as Step 1—a daily morning capsule covering four mechanisms with full label disclosure. Run it for 4–8 weeks before adding complexity. Consistency on one product tells you more than simultaneous introduction of five.

## Add mechanisms, not ingredients

Step 2 (Cellular Energy) addresses NAD+ pathway maintenance. Step 3 (Recovery) addresses mitochondrial function. Each step adds a distinct mechanism—not another version of the same polyphenol.

## Timing matters less than consistency

Morning compounds (Foundation, Cellular Energy) and evening compounds (Recovery) are separated by design. But perfect timing to the minute matters less than taking them daily for months.

## Evaluate on the right timeline

Cellular processes do not produce 14-day transformations. If a brand promises one, ask for the citation. We build for years—a protocol designed to compound, not to spike.

## Replace, don't accumulate

When you add PSL products, audit what you're already taking. Overlapping B vitamins, duplicate NAD+ precursors, or redundant antioxidants add cost and confusion without adding mechanism.
