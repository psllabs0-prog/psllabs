"Third-party tested" has become a vague claim on many product labels. Suppliers say it. Few publish the results.

## What third-party actually means

Third-party means a laboratory independent of the manufacturer conducted the analysis. Ideally:

- The lab is ISO 17025 accredited
- The manufacturer does not own or control the lab
- Full results are published—not a pass/fail badge

In-house testing is not third-party testing. "Tested at our facility" is not third-party testing.

## What gets tested

A meaningful panel includes:

- **Identity** — Is the compound correct?
- **Potency** — Is it at label claim?
- **Contaminants** — Heavy metals, microbes

Testing only for "purity" without defining methods is incomplete. Testing only the cheapest ingredient in a blend is misleading.

## PSL's standard

Every PSL batch is tested by an independent accredited laboratory before release. COAs are published in full for every lot. If a batch fails any specification, it does not ship.

This is not a competitive advantage—it should be baseline. We publish anyway because most suppliers do not.
